Visualization module¶

The meep_adjoint package ships with a built-in visualization module to facilitate graphical inspection of material geometries and functional subregions (solver inputs) as well as EM field configurations and design-function gradients (solver outputs).

A first goal of this module is to provide a go-to visualization facility for designers to have “under their fingertips”, easily remembered and invoked at any stage of the design process to lend visual insight into what’s going on at that moment. This is realized by the visualize

https://meep.readthedocs.io/en/latest/Python_User_Interface/#data-visualization

More specifically, the visualization module knows how to produce three types of plots:

  1. Geometry plots, showing the material configuration throughout the computational cell of the problem, the PML layers, the finite-element mesh used to define the basis set (for problems using a FiniteElementBasis), and significant subregions such as source regions, objective regions, and the design region.

  2. Forward field plots, showing the results of forward timestepping calculations, particularly the spatial distribution of energy fluxes and energy densities used to compute the objective function \(f^\text{obj}\).

  3. Adjoint derivative plots, showing the results of adjoint timestepping calculations—specifically, the quantity \(\frac{\partial f^\text{obj}}{\partial \epsilon(\mathbf x)}\) as a function of \(\mathbf{x}\) throughout the design region.
